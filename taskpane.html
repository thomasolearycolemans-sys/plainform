      How Plainform works

      Plainform checks your Word text against the Australian Government Style Manual and runs entirely inside Word — your text is analysed here and isn't sent anywhere.

      What it does for you

      • Word swaps — plain-language replacements with the tense kept right (commencing → starting). Click Apply.
      • Spelling — US → Australian (organize → organise). Now clickable too.
      • Apply all swaps — accept every word and spelling swap at once.

      What it points out (your call)

      • Long sentences and passive voice — clearer rewritten, but only you can keep the meaning.
      • Double negatives — not unreasonable; offered a one-click positive flip where it's clean.
      • Undefined acronyms — flagged so you define them on first use.
      • Numbers & dates — spell out one–nine, use 3 April 2025 not 3/4/2025.

      Marks in the document

      Issues are highlighted in light colours: turquoise word swap   yellow spelling   green sentence, acronym or number.

      Turn on Show explanations on words and each marked word gets a Word comment — hover or click the word in the document to see the issue and the recommended change. (Needs a newer Word version; if it's unavailable the highlights still work.)

      Use Clear marks to remove the highlights and Plainform's comments at any time.

      AI assistant (Gemini)

      Open the AI tab at the top to use this. Off by default. Plainform is offline and sends nothing anywhere — unless you turn on the AI assistant and click review on a passage. Turning it on asks you to confirm first. When on, only the paragraph you select is sent to Google Gemini (through your own relay, which holds the key) — never the whole document, and never as you type. Turn it off and nothing can be sent. Note: Google's free tier may use submitted text to improve their models, so use AI mode for non-sensitive text only.

      Tracked changes

      Turn on Apply as tracked changes and every swap you apply becomes a real Word tracked change a reviewer can accept or reject — useful for documents that go through review.

      Audit log

      Export audit log (CSV) saves a row-by-row record of every change you applied, ignored or reviewed — handy for a workpaper or to show a reviewer what was altered and why.

      Tips

      • Press Scan now any time, or leave Scan as I type on.
      • Ignore on a suggestion hides that word for the rest of the session.
